Motivation

Setting up a connected Wireless network around a boundary

Robots of communication/sensing radii $d$ and diameter $R$ attach using SCS.

  • What is the probability that they form a connected network?
  • How many robots ensure $95\%$ chance of connectivity?

Motivation

Handling noise in Deterministic Coverage Schemes

Robots attach at equidistant positions, but have odometric noise.

  • What guarantees can we give about connectivity?
  • What can we say about robot pdfs?
    Complicated mixture of Gaussians!
  • What happens for robots with different noise profiles?
    PDFs still harder to compute!

Objectives

Given the coverage scheme, robot details ($\dia,\conn$), and the boundary. Provide the designer with tools to:

  • Compute the probability of connectivity of the communication network.
  • Compute spatial pdfs and inter-robot distances.
  • Design the number of robots $\nor$ and communication range $\conn$ to satisfy a given connectivity probability.

Problem Setting

  • Similar to multi-boundary coverage, except we have only one line segment boundary $\mathcal{B}$
  • $\mathcal{B}$ has one end coloured
  • Robots attach to $\Bo$ using a programmed SCS
    Could be just a DCS + noise

Robot Capabilities

  • Sense and communicate within $\conn$
  • Detect coloured endpoint of $\Bo$
  • Know id of self and others
  • Measure distances on $\Bo$ using odometry

Robot Configuration

  • Boundary has length $s$ measured by odometry
  • Position of $\Rob{i}$ = distance of left end from coloured endpoint = $\upos{i}$ on $\Bo$
  • Configuration = unordered positions $\uposvec = \upos{1:n}$
  • Configuration is collision-free (CF) iff $|\upos{i} - \upos{j}| \geq \delta$

Robot communication

$\Rob{i}$ and $\Rob{j}$ communicate iff $|\upos{i} - \upos{j} | \leq \conn$

Communication Graph $\mathcal{G}$

Define the communication graph $\mathcal{G}$ as one with

  • vertices at each position $\upos{i}$
  • edges connecting pairs of inter-communicating robots

For theoretical convenience, assume $\Rob{0}=-\dia$ and $\Rob{n+1}=s$ attach at endpoints of $\Bo$.

Communication Graph $\mathcal{G}$

Several connected communication graphs are possible: \begin{align} \G&=\{(0,1),(1,3),(2,3),(2,4)\} \\ \G&=\{(0,1),(1,3),(1,2),(2,3),(3,4)\}. \end{align}

Order Statistics

    Order Statistics : Sorted positions $\posvec = \pos{1:n}$ (David'03)
  • $\uposvec$ is called parent of $\posvec$
  • Each $\upos{i}$ samples a r.v $\rvupos{i}$ with parent pdf $\fpar{i}$ suppoted on $\Bo$
  • Joint pdf of $\rvuposvec$ specifies the SCS



Slacks

  • Slacks are interrobot distances $\si{i} :=\pos{i} - \pos{i-1}$
  • Slack vector $\svec := \si{1:n+1}$
  • CF requires $\si{i} \geq \dia$
  • Connectivity requires $\si{i} \leq \conn$
  • Slack samples the rv $\rvsla{i}$ realized by $\rvuposvec$

Problem Statement - I

Suppose $\upos{i}$ are realizations of iid parents $\rvupos{i}$ each with pdf $\fpar(t)$ supported on $\Bo=\{t:0\leq t\leq s\}$.

Determine:

  • Probability $\pcon$ that $\G$ is connected.
  • PDFs of order statistics and slacks.

Problem Statement - II

Extend the analysis in Part I to the cases when:

  • robots have distinct communication radii $d_i$
  • parents $\rvuposvec$ are inid

Determine the complexity of computing $\pcon$.

Position Simplex

$$ \newcommand{R}{\mathbb{R}} \newcommand{\psimp}{\mathcal{P}} $$

Consider point robots, for which CF is irrelevant

  • Think of $\posvec$ (and $\uposvec$) as a point in $\R^n$
  • Valid (sorted) positions obey the inequalities $$ \pos{0} = 0\leq \pos{1} \leq \ldots \pos{n} \leq \pos{n+1} =s $$
  • Set of such positions forms position simplex $\psimp$
  • $\psimp$ has vertices of the form $(0,0,\ldots,0,s,s,\ldots,s)$

Slack Simplex

$$ \newcommand{\ssimp}{\mathcal{S}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} $$
  • Think of $\svec$ as a point in $\R^{n+1}$
  • Valid slack vectors have $$ \si{i} \geq 0, \text{and} \\ \begin{cases} \sum_{i=1}^{n+1} \si{i} = s, \\ \text{or}~ \sum_{i=1}^{n} \si{i} \leq s \end{cases}. $$
  • Set of such vectors forms slack simplex $\ssimp$
  • $\ssimp$ is a regular simplex of side $s\sqrt{2}$

Defining Connectivity

$$ \newcommand{\hyp}{\mathcal{H}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} $$
  • Connected slack vectors have $0 \leq \si{i} \leq \conn$
  • All these vectors lie in a hypercube $$ \hyp := \{ \svec \in \R^{n+1} : 0\leq \si{i} \leq \conn \} $$

Connected and Disconnected Regions

$$ \newcommand{\hyp}{\mathcal{H}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} \newcommand{\fav}{\mathcal{F}} \newcommand{\unfav}{\mathcal{U}} \newcommand{\vvec}{\mathbf{v}} $$
  • For $\G%$ to be connected, we need $\svec \in \ssimp \cap \hyp$
  • Define the connected region $\fav := \ssimp \cap \hyp$
  • Define the disconnected region $\unfav := \ssimp \setminus \fav$

Decomposing $\unfav$

$$ \newcommand{\hyp}{\mathcal{H}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} \newcommand{\fav}{\mathcal{F}} \newcommand{\unfav}{\mathcal{U}} \newcommand{\vvec}{\mathbf{v}} $$

Theorem ( Kumar and Berman ) The disconnected region can be expressed as the intersection of simplices $$ \unfav := \bigcap\limits_{\vvec \in \{0,1 \} ^{n+1}, \1^T \vvec \geq 1} \ssimp(\vvec), $$ where $$ \begin{align} \label{eqn:decomp} \ssimp(\vvec):= \{ \svec \in \ssimp : s_i \geq \conn ~\text{whenever}~ v_i =1 \}. \end{align} $$

$\ssimp(\vvec)$ forms a regular simplex of side $s-d\1^T\vvec$.

Summary of Analytical Results

Uniform Coverage: Finding $\pcon(\G)$

$$ \newcommand{\Vol}{\mathrm{Vol}} \newcommand{\nmin}{n_{\min}} $$

When parents $\rvupos{i}$ are each uniform on $\Bo$, we have $$ \pcon(\G) = 1 - \sum _{k=1} ^{\nmin} (-1)^{k-1} \binom{n}{k} {(1-\frac{kd}{s})^n } ~\text{where}~ \nmin = \lfloor \frac{s}{\conn} \rfloor. $$

Here, $\nmin$ is the minimum number of robots needed for connectivity.

IID Coverage: Finding $\pcon(\G)$

$$ \newcommand{\rvsvec}{\mathbf{S}} \newcommand{\jsla}{f_{\rvsvec}(\svec)} $$
  • When parents $\rvupos{i}$ are each iid on $\Bo$, we have $$ \pcon(\G) = \frac{\int_{\svec \in \fav} \jsla d\svec }{\int_{\svec \in \ssimp} \jsla d\svec }. $$
  • No easy way to do this in general!
  • Can be simplified using the probability integral transform $Y'_i := \Fpar_{X'_i}(t)$.

IID Coverage: Order Statistics

  • For general iid parents, (David '03) $$ f_{\rvposvec}(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = h(x_1)h(x_2)\ldots h(x_n) ~\text{over}~\psimp, \\ f_{\rvpos{i}}(t) = \sum \limits_{j=i}^n \Fpar(t)^j (1-\Fpar(t))^{n-j}. $$
  • Slacks have $$f_{ \rvsla{i}} (t) = \frac{n!}{(i-1)! (n-i-1)!} \times \\ \int_{x=0} ^{s} {\Fpar(x)}^{i-1} \fpar(x) \fpar(x+t) ( 1 - \Fpar(x+t))^{n-i-1} dx.$$

IID Coverage: Distinct communication radii

  • Suppose the communication radii are $(d_i)_{i=1:n}$.
  • The hypercube $\hyp$ needs to be replaced by a union of hypercuboids, $$ \hyp' := [0,d_1] \times [0,d_1] \times \ldots [0,d_{n/2}] \times [0,d_{n/2}] $$
  • Computing $\pcon(\G)$ is provably hard :
    Complexity Section
  • No difference to order statistics.

Finite Robots with IID Parents

$$ \newcommand{\Vol}{\mathrm{Vol}} \newcommand{\nmin}{n_{\min}} $$

When robots have diameter $\dia$, use $\ssimp_{CF}$ instead of $\ssimp$ $$ \ssimp_{CF} := \{ \svec \in \ssimp: s_i \geq d \}. $$

  • No (simple) formula for any of $\rvpos{i}, S_i$, and $\pcon$!
  • Straightforward replacements of $\ssimp$ with $\ssimp_{CF}$ everywhere.

Point robots with INID parents

$$ \newcommand{\Vol}{\mathrm{Vol}} \newcommand{\nmin}{n_{\min}} $$
  • Joint pdf for iid parents no longer valid
  • Instead, Bapat-Beg theorem (David '03) gives the joint CDF as a matrix permanent.
  • Computing $\pcon$ is provably hard.

$$ \newcommand{\PCON} {\mathsf{PCON}} \newcommand{\Q}{\mathbb{Q}} $$

Lower bounds for $\pcon$

Define the $\PCON(\fpar)$ problem with

  • Inputs : $(s,d,n) \in \Q_+$ and $\fpar$ is the parent
  • Output : $\pcon(\G) \in \Q_+$

$\PCON(\mathrm{Uni})$ is Pseudo-$\mathsf{P}$

Theorem (Kumar and Berman, to appear) $\PCON(\mathrm{Uni})$ can be solved in $O(n \log n)$ time and $\Omega(n)$ time.

Proof : The upper bound comes from formula: $$ \pcon(\G) = 1 - \sum _{k=1} ^{\nmin} (-1)^{k-1} \binom{n}{k} {(1-\frac{kd}{s})^n } ~\text{where}~ \nmin = \lfloor \frac{s}{\conn} \rfloor. $$ The lower bound arises from the number of bits in $\pcon$ for which $s=4d/3$, so $\pcon \approx 1-n/4^n$, consuming $\Omega(n)$ bits.

$$ \newcommand{\PH}{\#\mathsf{PH}} $$

Computing the volume of simplex-hypercube intersection

Theorem (Dyer and Frieze '87)

Computing the volume of intersection between

  • a general simplex and the unit hypercube, and
  • a hypercuboid and the unit simplex,

are both $\PH$.

$$ \newcommand{\PH}{\#\mathsf{PH}} $$

$\PCON$ is $\PH$ for iid parents

  • Define an $n$-uniform pdf as follows. Divide the support $\Bo$ into $n$ subintervals, and set $f(t) = a_i$ when $t$ lies in the $i$-th subinterval.
  • Theorem (Kumar and Berman, to appear) $\PCON$ is $\PH$ for a uniform pdf of $n$ pieces supported on $\Bo$.
  • Proof : Reduction from (Dyer and Frieze '87) .

Other hard cases of $\PCON$

Theorem (Kumar and Berman, to appear) $\PCON$ is $\PH$ for the following instances:

  • Uniform parents of robots with distinct radii
  • General INID parents

Proof : Reduction from (Dyer and Frieze '87) .

Towards Realistic MultiRobot + Wireless Simulation

ROS

  • Excellent implementation of pose tracking, motion planning, etc.
  • Limited Multi-Robot simulation support.

ARGoS

  • Excellent Multi-Robot simulation
    • Up to 100,000 Robots supported (Pinciroli et al., 2011)
  • Little pose tracking, motion planning, etc.
  • RoboNetsim patches ARGoS-2 with NS-3 (Kudelski et al., 2013)
    • Does not work with ARGoS3!

GENI

  • Virtual networking lab, with support for MANET
  • Can be used for accurate Wi-fi experiments
  • Precludes need for simulation
  • Not obvious how to combine this with robots
