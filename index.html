SHS : Behavioral Model

CRN with 6 reactions $S_i \underset{r_{ij}}{\stackrel{r_{ji}}{\rightleftharpoons}} S_j$, where $S_i, S_j$ are distinct states from $S:=\{F,B,D\}$

SHS : Behavioral Model

  • Behavioral components = ant counts in each state $$\mathbf{N}_S := \begin{bmatrix} N_F & N_B & N_D \end{bmatrix}^T$$
  • 6 Transitions $S_i \rightarrow S_{j}$
    • Intensity : $r_{ij} N_i$
    • Reset Map: $(N_i,N_j) \mapsto (N_i-1,N_j+1)$

SHS : Dynamical Model

Free Body Diagram of load. Each front ant pulls with force $F_p$, and each attached ant lifts with force $F_l$. $$F_{up} = (N_F+N_B)F_l \\ F_n = m_L g -F_{up}$$.

SHS : Dynamical Model

  • Dynamical components : load position and velocity $x_L, v_L$
  • Attached ants pull with net force $F_{up} = (N_F+N_B)F_l$
  • Each front ant pulls with proportional regulation $$ F_{p} = K (v_L^D - v_L) $$
    • $K$ = proportional gain
    • $v_L^D$ = desired load velocity

Evolution of SHS

SHS State Vector

$$ \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} N_{F} & N_B & N_D & x_L & v_L \end{bmatrix}^T$$

Flow Equation

$$ \mathbf{\dot{x}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0& v_L & a_L \end{bmatrix}^T$$

Moment Dynamics

$$\newcommand {\Exp} {\mathbb{E}}$$ Population Counts $$ \frac{d}{dt} \Exp(N_{i:i\in S}) = \sum_{\substack{j \in S, j \neq i}} \left( r_{ji} \Exp(N_j) - r_{ij} \Exp(N_i) \right) $$ Load Position $$\frac{d}{dt} \Exp(x_L) = \Exp( v_L )$$
$$\newcommand{eqdef}{:=}$$

Moment Dynamics

$$\newcommand {\Exp} {\mathbb{E}}$$ Load Velocity

$$ \frac{d}{dt} \Exp(v_L) \approx c_g + c_F \Exp(N_F) + c_B \Exp(N_B) + c_{Fv} \Exp( N_F ) \Exp( v_L ) $$

where $$ \begin{align} c_g \eqdef -\mu g &,& c_B \eqdef \mu F_l/m_L \\ c_F \eqdef (K v_L^d + \mu F_l)/m_L &,& c_{Fv} \eqdef K/m_L \end{align} $$.

Problem Statement: Objective

  • Small, with 1 robots / disk

Assumptions: Robot Capabilities

Assumptions: Disk Properties

  • Types distinguished by weight, colour, etc.

Robot Controller

Microscopic Model: Bimolecular CRN

    Species
  • Free robots $r$
  • Bound zones $B$
  • Unbound zones $U$
    Reactions
  • Bind $r + U \xrightarrow{p_b e_u} B$
  • Unbind $r+B \xrightarrow {p_u e_b} 2r+U$

The robot ordering unbinding acts as an enzyme.

Encounter rates

$e_b~(\text{resp.} ~e_u)$: Probability rate at which free robot encounters bound (resp. unbound) zone

  • Hard to derive from first principles!
  • Not under designer's control.

Binding and Unbinding probabilities

$p_b~(\text{resp.} ~p_u)$: Probability that a free robot will bind (resp. ask a bound robot to unbind)

Under designer's control

Macroscopic ODE Model

Describes the time evolution of species concentrations $$\mathbf{x} := \begin{bmatrix} r & U & B \end{bmatrix}^T$$

ODE System $$ \dot{\mathbf{x}} = r(p_u e_b B - p_b e_u U) \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}^T , ~\text{with}~ \mathbf{x}_0 ~ \text{given}. $$

Equilibria

ODE has two equilibria of the form

$$ \mathbf{x}^* = (r^*,U^*,B^*), ~\text{with}~ B^* + U^* = B_0^* +U_0^*. $$

Trivial Equilibrium

If $r_0 +B_0 < B^*$, then we have

$(r^*,U^*,b^*) = (0, U_0 -r_0, B_0+r_0)$

caused by free robot depletion.

Nontrivial equilibrium

When $r_0 + B_0 > B^*$,

\begin{align}r^* &= (r_0 +B_0) (1- \frac{B^*}{B_0+U_0}) -(U_0-r_0) \frac{B^*}{B_0+U_0} \\ \frac{B^*}{U^*} &= \frac{p_be_u}{p_ue_b} \\ B^*+U^* &= B_0+U_0 \end{align}

with a unique solution.

Unimolecular approximation

When $r_0$ is very large, $r(t) \approx r(0)$, and we approximate the CRN by

\begin{align} U \xrightarrow {p_b e_u r_0} B \\ B \xrightarrow {p_u e_b r_0} U \end{align}

with time constant $\tau = \frac{1}{r_0(p_b e_u + p_u e_b)}$.

Correction for Spatial Effects

Correction for Spatial Effects

  • In practice, we get less than $100\%$ allocation on disk.
  • Let $a$ be the avoidance distance between robots.
  • The $\delta(a)$ function corrects for nonideal allocation: $$ \frac{B^*}{U^* + B^*} = \frac{1}{1 + \delta} \overbrace{\frac{\frac{p_b}{p_u}}{ \frac{p_b}{p_u} + \frac{e_b}{e_u} }}^{{\text{Idealized allocation}}} $$

CONTROL POLICY

Given $\mathbf{x}_0, \mathbf{x}^*$ and $e_b/e_u$, the policy with

$$ (p_b, p_u) = \begin{cases} \left(\, \frac{e_bB^*}{e_uU^*} \frac{(1 + \delta)}{1 - \delta B^*/U^*}, 1\,\right) & \text{if } e_b B^* (1 + \delta) < e_u U^* (1 - \delta \frac{B^*}{U^*}),\\ \left(\,1, \frac{e_u}{e_b} \frac{U^*}{B^*} \frac{1 - \delta B^*/U^*}{(1 + \delta)}\,\right) & \text{otherwise}. \end{cases} $$ converges fastest to equilibrium.

Validation of Control Strategy

Example of a single simulated trajectory of the control policy, allocating 300 robots allocating to 3 disks. The $(p_b,p_u)$ policy was chosen to achieve a mean $B/(B+U)$ allocation of $37.5\%$ shown as a dashed horizontal line.

Average of 10 simulated trajectories of the control policy, allocating 300 robots allocating to 3 disks. The $(p_b,p_u)$ policy was chosen to achieve a mean $B/(B+U)$ allocation of $37.5\%$ shown as a dashed horizontal line.

Motivation

  • How many robots ensure $95\%$ chance of connectivity?

  • What happens for robots with different noise profiles?

Problem Setting

  • Similar to multi-boundary coverage, except we have only one line segment boundary $\mathcal{B}$
  • $\mathcal{B}$ has one end coloured
  • Robots attach to $\Bo$ using a programmed SCS
    Could be just a DCS + noise

Robot Capabilities

  • Sense and communicate within $\conn$
  • Detect coloured endpoint of $\Bo$
  • Know id of self and others
  • Measure distances on $\Bo$ using odometry

Robot Configuration

  • Boundary has length $s$ measured by odometry
  • Position of $\Rob{i}$ = distance of left end from coloured endpoint = $\upos{i}$ on $\Bo$
  • Configuration = unordered positions $\uposvec = \upos{1:n}$
  • Configuration is collision-free (CF) iff $|\upos{i} - \upos{j}| \geq \delta$

Robot communication

$\Rob{i}$ and $\Rob{j}$ communicate iff $|\upos{i} - \upos{j} | \leq \conn$

Communication Graph $\mathcal{G}$

Define the communication graph $\mathcal{G}$ as one with

  • vertices at each position $\upos{i}$
  • edges connecting pairs of inter-communicating robots

For theoretical convenience, assume $\Rob{0}=-\dia$ and $\Rob{n+1}=s$ attach at endpoints of $\Bo$.

Communication Graph $\mathcal{G}$

Several connected communication graphs are possible: \begin{align} \G&=\{(0,1),(1,3),(2,3),(2,4)\} \\ \G&=\{(0,1),(1,3),(1,2),(2,3),(3,4)\}. \end{align}

Order Statistics

    Order Statistics : Sorted positions $\posvec = \pos{1:n}$ (David'03)
  • $\uposvec$ is called parent of $\posvec$
  • Each $\upos{i}$ samples a r.v $\rvupos{i}$ with parent pdf $\fpar{i}$ suppoted on $\Bo$
  • Joint pdf of $\rvuposvec$ specifies the SCS

Slacks

  • Slacks are interrobot distances $\si{i} :=\pos{i} - \pos{i-1}$
  • Slack vector $\svec := \si{1:n+1}$
  • CF requires $\si{i} \geq \dia$
  • Connectivity requires $\si{i} \leq \conn$
  • Slack samples the rv $\rvsla{i}$ realized by $\rvuposvec$

Problem Statement - I

Suppose $\upos{i}$ are realizations of iid parents $\rvupos{i}$ each with pdf $\fpar(t)$ supported on $\Bo=\{t:0\leq t\leq s\}$.

Determine:

  • Probability $\pcon$ that $\G$ is connected.
  • PDFs of order statistics and slacks.

Problem Statement - II

Extend the analysis in Part I to the cases when:

  • robots have distinct communication radii $d_i$
  • parents $\rvuposvec$ are inid

Determine the complexity of computing $\pcon$.

Problem Solving Tools

  • Geometric interpretation of $\posvec$ and $\svec$
  • Order Statistics
  • Polytopes
  • Complexity of simplex/hypercube intersection

Communication Graph $\mathcal{G}$

  • $\G$ is a Geometric Graph
  • When $\upos{i}$ are drawn from iid rv's, $G$ is a Random Geometric Graph (RGG) (Penrose '03)
  • $\G$ is Gilbert's Disk Graph (Haenggi '12) in $\mathbb{R}^1$

Position Simplex

$$ \newcommand{R}{\mathbb{R}} \newcommand{\psimp}{\mathcal{P}} $$

Consider point robots, for which CF is irrelevant

  • Think of $\posvec$ (and $\uposvec$) as a point in $\R^n$
  • Valid (sorted) positions have $$ \pos{0} = 0\leq \pos{1} \leq \ldots \pos{n} \leq \pos{n+1} =s $$
  • Set of such positions forms position simplex $\psimp$
  • Vertices of $\psimp$ are of the form $$(0,0,\ldots,0,s,s,\ldots,s)$$

Slack Simplex

$$ \newcommand{\ssimp}{\mathcal{S}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} $$
  • Think of $\svec$ as a point in $\R^{n+1}$
  • Valid slack vectors have $$ \begin{align} \si{i} \geq 0 \\ \sum_{i=1}^{n+1} \si{i} = s, ~\text{or as a half space}~ \sum_{i=1}^{n} \si{i} \leq s. \end{align} $$
  • Set of such vectors forms slack simplex $\ssimp$

Defining Connectivity

$$ \newcommand{\hyp}{\mathcal{hyp}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} $$
  • Connected slack vectors have $0 \leq \si{i} \leq \conn$
  • All these vectors lie in a hypercube $$ \hyp := \{ \svec \in \R^{n+1} : 0\leq \si{i} \leq \conn \} $$
  • This is immaterial of $\svec$ lying in $\ssimp$.

Connected and Disconnected Regions

$$ \newcommand{\hyp}{\mathcal{H}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} \newcommand{\fav}{\mathcal{F}} \newcommand{\unfav}{\mathcal{U}} \newcommand{\vvec}{\mathbf{v}} $$
  • For $\G%$ to be connected, we need $\svec \in \ssimp \cap \hyp$
  • Define the connected region $\fav := \ssimp \cap \hyp$
  • Define the disconnected region $\unfav := \ssimp \setminus \fav$

Decomposing $\unfav$

$$ \newcommand{\hyp}{\mathcal{H}} \newcommand{\1}{\mathbf{1}} \newcommand{\fav}{\mathcal{F}} \newcommand{\unfav}{\mathcal{U}} \newcommand{\vvec}{\mathbf{v}} $$

Theorem ( Kumar and Berman ) The disconnected region can be expressed as the intersection of simplices $$ \unfav := \bigcap\limits_{\vvec \in \{0,1 \} ^{n+1}, \1^T \vvec \geq 1} \ssimp(\vvec), $$ where $$ \begin{align} \label{eqn:decomp} \ssimp(\vvec):= \{ \svec \in \ssimp : s_i \geq \conn ~\text{whenever}~ v_i =1 \}. \end{align} $$

$\ssimp(\vvec)$ forms a simplex of side $s-d\1^T\vvec$.

Summary of Results

Uniform Coverage: Finding $\pcon(\G)$

$$ \newcommand{\Vol}{\mathrm{Vol}} \newcommand{\nmin}{n_{\min}} $$

When parents $\rvupos{i}$ are each uniform on $\Bo$, we have $$ \pcon(\G) = 1 - \sum _{k=1} ^{\nmin} (-1)^{k-1} \binom{n}{k} {(1-kd/s)^n } ~\text{where}~ \nmin = \lfloor \frac{s}{\conn} \rfloor. $$

Here, $\nmin$ is the minimum number of robots needed for connectivity.

IID Coverage: Finding $\pcon(\G)$

$$ \newcommand{\rvsvec}{\mathbf{S}} \newcommand{\jsla}{f_{\rvsvec}(\svec)} $$
  • When parents $\rvupos{i}$ are each iid on $\Bo$, we have $$ \pcon(\G) = \frac{\int_{\svec \in \fav} \jsla d\svec }{\int_{\svec \in \ssimp} \jsla d\svec }. $$
  • No easy way to do this in general!
  • Can be simplified using the probability integral transform $Y'_i := \Fpar_{X'_i}(t)$.

IID Coverage: Order Statistics

  • For general $\G$ (David & Nagaraja '03) $$ f_{\rvpos{i}}(t) = \sum \limits_{j=i}^n \Fpar(t)^j (1-\Fpar(t))^{n-j}. $$
  • For connected $\G$, we need to marginalize similarly to $\pcon(\G)$.
  • Formulas for slacks found by a similar process.

Finite Robots with IID Coverage

$$ \newcommand{\Vol}{\mathrm{Vol}} \newcommand{\nmin}{n_{\min}} $$

When robots have diameter $\dia$, use $\ssimp_{CF}$ instead of $\ssimp$ $$ \ssimp_{CF} := \{ \svec \in \ssimp: s_i \geq d \}. $$

No (simple) formula for any of $\rvpos{i}, S_i$, and $\pcon$!

Lower bounds for $\pcon$

Define the $\PCON(\fpar)$ problem with

  • Inputs : $(s,d,n) \in \Q_+$ and $\fpar$ is the parent
  • Output : $\pcon(\G) \in \Q_+$

$\PCON(\mathrm{Uni})$ is $\mathrm{Psuedo}-\mathsf{P}$

Theorem (Kumar and Berman, to appear) $\PCON(\mathrm{Uni})$ can be solved in $\Omega(n)$ and $O(n \log n)$ time.

Proof : Straightforward from formula.

$$ \newcommand{\PH}{\#\mathsf{PH}} $$

$\PCON(\mathrm{poly})$ is $\PH$

Theorem (Kumar and Berman, to appear) $\PCON$ is $\PH$ for arbitrary polynomial parents supported on $\Bo$.

Proof : Reduction from (Dyer and Frieze '87) .
